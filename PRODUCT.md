# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Journalists, policy and governance people, researchers, public servants, and
civically-curious Australians who want to know how Commonwealth agencies
describe their use of AI. They arrive from a link or a search, usually to answer
a specific question ("has agency X published?", "what changed?", "who copied the
template?"), and they need to trust the answer enough to cite it.

## Product Purpose

A faithful, versioned, public record of every Australian Government AI
transparency statement, scraped daily and committed to git, with analysis layered
on top: a timeline of every change, a similarity map, and a propagation explorer
that shows how much of each statement is bespoke versus copied from the DTA
template. Success is when a reader can find the relevant fact, see exactly what
changed and when, and follow it back to the original source with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Sharp civic-tech: contemporary, precise, quietly witty, with a point of view.
It reads government documents critically so you don't have to. Authoritative
because it shows its working (git history, traceable figures), not because it
postures. Dry humour over hype. A public-interest project from a cybernetics
studio, not a vendor.

## Anti-references

Corporate SaaS landing pages above all: no hero-metric template (big gradient
number, three supporting stats), no endless identical feature-card grids, no
gradient-filled accents, no marketing buzzwords (streamline / empower /
transform / seamless). Also not the stock gov.au template look, and not an arty
editorial-magazine pastiche (display-serif italics, drop caps, broadsheet grid)
that would undercut the civic-tech credibility.

## Design Principles

- **On the record.** Authority comes from being a faithful, timestamped,
  git-backed record. Keep figures traceable to a source; feel evidentiary and
  precise, not editorialised beyond what the data supports.
- **Mark up the government's homework.** Lead the eye to what changed, what's
  copied, and what's bespoke. The point of view is a critical public-interest
  reading, not a neutral mirror.
- **Show the interesting thing.** The data is genuinely interesting; dramatise
  the standout facts instead of burying them in muted small print.
- **Credible, not corporate.** Sharp and contemporary with dry wit; never the
  SaaS-landing clichés listed in anti-references.
- **Legible first.** Data journalism lives or dies on readability: keep AA
  contrast, reduced-motion alternatives, semantic HTML, and fast static
  delivery.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large/bold text ≥3:1, including over
tinted highlight backgrounds. Honour `prefers-reduced-motion` with crossfade or
instant alternatives for every animation. Keep semantic HTML, visible focus
states, a skip link, and the existing light/dark support via `color-scheme`.
Colour is never the sole carrier of meaning (diffs, heat levels, and status all
pair colour with text or shape).
